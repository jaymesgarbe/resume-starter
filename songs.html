Overall Review

Last night Google News greeted me with Pitchfork's review of Buck Meek's recently-released solo album, "Haunted Mountain". With an impressive rating of 7.2, I just HAD to give it a listen (though I usually take Pitchfork's ratings with a grain of salt). This album feels almost like an extension of his band's work, something I would never disapprove of. This is my favorite track so far, the title track is also really good. Give this one a listen and please listen to the whole album if you're into it!! Make it a great week.

Album Review

SOS - SZA

This week I sat terrified at how a new album after a hiatus of 5 years could possibly compete with what I consider the gold-standard of modern R&B records, "Ctrl". SZA answered on Friday with an eclectic diary-like record of ranging emotions over ranging genres with a tracklist nearly doubling her triple platinum sophomore release. SZA diverts the path of expectations in such a grandiose fashion that it is impossible to collate these projects. The constant shifting of moods and genres (and the fact that she does them all so masterfully) keep the hefty 23-song tracklist from becoming monotonous. As expected, her off-the-shoulder songwriting amazes on every track through which she is able to maintain a few common motifs throughout. Her lyrics keep the album from sounding like a collection of randomly produced songs, a fault to which many other experimental albums fall victim. Most have thoughtlessly labeled this album as R&B/Pop due to SZA's past successes in this genre. I now struggle to consider SZA solely an R&B artist and find it completely unjust to slap this common epithet on "SOS". This album displays expansive talent in sonic styles never before heard from SZA and I just hope it can sate me for the time between now and her next project. Thank you.

Celebrity Song of the Week

Kinzey: Pasture Child - Dominic Fike

Overall Rating 8.4/10

Listen Here

Overall Review

I’ve recently been listening to Dominic Fike’s new album on repeat (especially since I saw him in concert!). I could have chosen virtually any song from this album to be my song of the week, but I have a particular love for this one lately. The play-on-words title refers to a childhood crush or lover who is said to have had a sheltered upbringing, but grows up to do big things. Fike reminisces about that relationship in a time before their lives became more complicated, and he introduces an additional sentiment about the way that technology has impacted our lives and interactions with others. The production has a lot of subtle detail, and I really love the guitar parts throughout this whole album. I find this song really sweet and the lyrics always make me smile.
