Song of the Week

Week 38: Skyline To - Frank Ocean

Overall Rating 9.4/10

Listen Here

Rate this song!

/10



Fav Part of Song

While I usually pick out a specific sonic element to praise for this section, my favorite part about this song is the way it makes me feel. It's every collective summer night passing by, but finding a moment of peace in that rapid passing of time. I can't explain why it has this effect on me, but I hope you feel it too.

Overall Review

Firstly, the bridge halfway into this song sounds like a Mort Garson composition, listen to any song from "Mother Earth's Plantasia", it's so etherial. As far as this track goes, I could talk for hours. Although it's not contending for a spot in the top 5 tracks from "Blonde" (10/10 album) because of its one-note nature, it does that one note superbly. "Summer's not as long as it used to be" is not only my favorite lyric from this song, but also sums up its storyline pretty well. The distortion of time is felt with both the lyrics and melody, both desired and undesired. This distortion is particularly effective in my impatient waiting for summer. As I listen to "Skyline To" repeatedly while writing this review, I'm also repeatingly talking myself down from the massive FOMO of missing Frank Ocean's Coachella performance in about 5 hours. Listen to this song, you will understand.

Album Review

SOS - SZA

This week I sat terrified at how a new album after a hiatus of 5 years could possibly compete with what I consider the gold-standard of modern R&B records, "Ctrl". SZA answered on Friday with an eclectic diary-like record of ranging emotions over ranging genres with a tracklist nearly doubling her triple platinum sophomore release. SZA diverts the path of expectations in such a grandiose fashion that it is impossible to collate these projects. The constant shifting of moods and genres (and the fact that she does them all so masterfully) keep the hefty 23-song tracklist from becoming monotonous. As expected, her off-the-shoulder songwriting amazes on every track through which she is able to maintain a few common motifs throughout. Her lyrics keep the album from sounding like a collection of randomly produced songs, a fault to which many other experimental albums fall victim. Most have thoughtlessly labeled this album as R&B/Pop due to SZA's past successes in this genre. I now struggle to consider SZA solely an R&B artist and find it completely unjust to slap this common epithet on "SOS". This album displays expansive talent in sonic styles never before heard from SZA and I just hope it can sate me for the time between now and her next project. Thank you.

Celebrity Song of the Week

Dallin Corless: Wet - Dazey and the Scouts

Overall Rating 7.9/10

Listen Here

Overall Review

Last time I did csotw, I reported on maybe one of the best songs ever written. So this time I figured id just cover this silly little bop I've been back into recently (lk thanks to a tiktok micro trend). I love this song because you can really feel the amount of fun the artists had recording it. It manages to feel moody and a little expressive while still remaining light hearted and upbeat thru its use of raunchy metaphors and catchy melodies, mimicking the sound of crying during the chorus. Lyrically, it just doesn't take itself that seriously, almost adapting a dissociative, laissez faire attitude towards the perils of a relationship. Sonically the song is aggressively fast paced, always keeping your interest. The song really does feel like that classic alt/punk pop an angsty protagonist would make in a coming of age movie and I love it.
