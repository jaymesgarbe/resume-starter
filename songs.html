Fav Part of Song

Fiona Apple's voice is too good honestly. She transforms this cover in a way that not even the BEATLES could. To give the boys credit, though, the lyricism of this song is really powerful to me as well.

Overall Review

This is one of very few covers that I prefer over the original. The Beatles' original rendition of this song honestly doesn't impress me in any way but lyrically. I think Fiona Apple brings out the existentiality of this song through her vocal whimsy with which I've fallen so in love. Give this one a listen and, if you like it, queue up my favorite Fiona Apple album "When The Pawn...". Make it a great week!

Album Review

SOS - SZA

This week I sat terrified at how a new album after a hiatus of 5 years could possibly compete with what I consider the gold-standard of modern R&B records, "Ctrl". SZA answered on Friday with an eclectic diary-like record of ranging emotions over ranging genres with a tracklist nearly doubling her triple platinum sophomore release. SZA diverts the path of expectations in such a grandiose fashion that it is impossible to collate these projects. The constant shifting of moods and genres (and the fact that she does them all so masterfully) keep the hefty 23-song tracklist from becoming monotonous. As expected, her off-the-shoulder songwriting amazes on every track through which she is able to maintain a few common motifs throughout. Her lyrics keep the album from sounding like a collection of randomly produced songs, a fault to which many other experimental albums fall victim. Most have thoughtlessly labeled this album as R&B/Pop due to SZA's past successes in this genre. I now struggle to consider SZA solely an R&B artist and find it completely unjust to slap this common epithet on "SOS". This album displays expansive talent in sonic styles never before heard from SZA and I just hope it can sate me for the time between now and her next project. Thank you.

Celebrity Song of the Week

Dallin Corless: anything - Adrianne Lenker

Overall Rating 9.5/10

Listen Here

Overall Review

This song was actually in the running for my last sotw, so I am overjoyed it's getting its moment. I think this song has some of the best lyrical songwriting I've ever heard. Lenker so effortlessly expresses the beauty in the mundane parts of our lives, and the desire to share that with someone. She explores this feeling of wanting to be so connected to someone, you are aware of all the parts of their lives that normally only they are privy to. Phrases like "wanna listen to the sound of you blinking" drive this idea of sharing your whole existence with someone. Almost every line from this song could stand alone as perfect little pieces of poetry, yet their assimilation into the work as a whole makes them all the more memorable. Sonically this song is almost synesthetic for me, evoking memories of my time in the outdoors with waterfalls, meadows, and a simpler existence. It may not be the flashiest genre of song writing, but this final product is just so innately beautiful and powerful to me.
