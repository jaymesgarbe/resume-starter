Song of the Week

Week 16: No L's - Smino

Overall Rating: 8.1/10

Listen Here

Rate this song!

/10



Fav Part of Song

Smino mentions Frank Ocean near the end of this track and subsequently, a snippet of Ocean's "Nikes" plays in the background. I appreciate the reference.

Overall Review

This track from Smino's very recently released album caught my attention immediately. Right away I recognized a familiar sampling of Monica's "Knock Knock", consistent with the background melody in "Potato Salad" by Tyler, The Creator. "No L's" feels like a more mellow and fluid parallel to the aformentioned track. This is one of my limited exposures to Smino, but he brings such an interestingly calm aura to this song and the rest of his album. Give this song a listen and make the best of your week. Until next time.

Celebrity Song of the Week

Lauren Rappa: Timefighter - Lucy Dacus

Overall Rating: 9.9/10

Listen Here

Overall Review

I can so vividly remember the first time I heard this song and how immediately taken I was with it. I love Lucy Dacusâ€™ music so much because I find her to be a very comforting presence. I believe that she is one of the greatest lyricists of our generation, and my personal favorite. Her voice is so soothing and beautiful. One of my favorite things about her music is how well she is able to perfectly curate specific vibes and feelings for each of her songs and albums as a whole cohesive piece. Her second album, Home Video, has a very nostalgic feeling. This album, Historians, has a very dark vibe that I really enjoy listening to. I think this is the best on the album because of how much of an out of body experience listening is for me. It discusses time passing and the feeling of trying to fight against the fleeting moments we have, and the inability of humans to stop the passage of time. The way that the song's pace increases tension and builds the feeling of anxiety that comes with the fear of aging and missing moments is so visceral, and is such a good representation of our lack of control of existence and its impermanence. That paired with the building intensity of the guitar and vocals is incredible. I find this song perfect to be honest but I was afraid to give it a 10/10 so I took it back to a 9.9 just to leave room.
