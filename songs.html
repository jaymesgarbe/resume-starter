Song of the Week

Week 26: Cologne - Beabadoobee

Overall Rating: 9.4/10

/10



Fav Part of Song

The buildups to the choruses distribute the pacing of this song and keep it from falling flat. Beabadoobee's voice is at its PEAK here and the lyrics are so amusing. There is so much to love about this song.

Overall Review

A year after the heat wave of success that was her hit single "death bed" in early 2020 and her closely followed album "Fake It Flowers", Beabadoobee subtly released an EP titled "Our Extended Play". Despite being a quaint 12 minutes long, the 4 tracks are cohesive and build on each other; they transport you. It is extensively reminiscent of early 2000's pop-punk (even the album cover). "Cologne" has this conceited overconfidence that encapsulates the pop-punk style. I love this kind of world-building in a song, it greatly heightens the experience. I hope you enjoy this one, and please make it a great week!

Album Review

SOS - SZA

This week I sat terrified at how a new album after a hiatus of 5 years could possibly compete with what I consider the gold-standard of modern R&B records, "Ctrl". SZA answered on Friday with an eclectic diary-like record of ranging emotions over ranging genres with a tracklist nearly doubling her triple platinum sophomore release. SZA diverts the path of expectations in such a grandiose fashion that it is impossible to collate these projects. The constant shifting of moods and genres (and the fact that she does them all so masterfully) keep the hefty 23-song tracklist from becoming monotonous. As expected, her off-the-shoulder songwriting amazes on every track through which she is able to maintain a few common motifs throughout. Her lyrics keep the album from sounding like a collection of randomly produced songs, a fault to which many other experimental albums fall victim. Most have thoughtlessly labeled this album as R&B/Pop due to SZA's past successes in this genre. I now struggle to consider SZA solely an R&B artist and find it completely unjust to slap this common epithet on "SOS". This album displays expansive talent in sonic styles never before heard from SZA and I just hope it can sate me for the time between now and her next project. Thank you.

Celebrity Song of the Week

Waylon Bell: Lil Thing - Knox Fortune

Overall Rating: 9.7/10

Listen Here

Overall Review

I discovered this song from the Cal Berkeley student radio about 2 years ago just in time for summer and it has not left my playlist ever since. While a 9.7 rating is incredibly high this song truly deserves it, it encapsulates the vibe of summer and especially summer love in a way that effortlessly floats up to your ears. The song was gorgeously produced with this funky beat that somehow sounds both retro and futuristic with the lyrics to tie it all together wonderfully. The lyrics definitely play into the summer vibe as the chorus reads “it’s the summer, and I’m at it again, she my lil thing, gets me outta my jam”. As you listen to this song you yearn for a day at the beach and if your lucky enough to be at the beach, well, you’re in for a treat. While I will be the first to admit I am far from knowing all of the intricacies of music and especially what correlates into good music so I will refrain from going in depth about the musical elements of this song but, that does not mean I do not know a good song when I hear one. This song has been one of my favorites for a long time and everyone I play it for always loves it too, so I ask you next time you’re on aux expand someone’s playlist with this one!
